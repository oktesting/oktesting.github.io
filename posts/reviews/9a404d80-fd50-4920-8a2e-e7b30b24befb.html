a movie with perfect balance of comedy and romance, not much of drama. all it does is to tell a story of growing up, maturing then self-realization. we are not and never be prepared of adulthood life, also we live a live that looks so much alike other, in a big moving fast city. the main character begin as completely clueless in life and then end his arc with something important that worth living the life for. his journey is purely fantasy (almost dream like to me), emotional and also inspirational. im not saying anyone should just quit their job and went to a remote area to live and work as a lumberjack like him but at least the movie is success at making us to dream a much more simpler but exciting than a dull life at big city for once.

the cinematography in this movie is very amazing. im so impressed by every shot they done in the woods. those tall green trees, the hill with people sitting and drinking freshly hot brewed coffee, the sun, the rain, the stream, the mist, etc.... man, i could just go over it all day.

comedy and romance are enough and well executed, we could just all forgive some illogical character's decisions they made in order to push the storyline forward.

almost at the end there are some dreamy scene, very much spiritual with mist slowly filling the forest and a hand catch then guide the Yuki. after that is the festival portion at the end which pretty much encapsulate the message that the filmmakers want to spread. the message is simple, take good care of our nature, learn to live harmony with it then pass the torch to the next generations with unique custom traditions.

one scene that i really like is when three characters in the ride home after the auction for the wood from the big tree that they had just taken down. one of them say, this business is kinda peculiar in its own way, in agriculture, we could see the result of our crop after sometime right away, but in forestry, we probably be all dead by the time evaluating our present work. we harvest our ancestor fruits as a privilege and our works are to grow and preserve for our predecessors with responsibility.
