The simpsons movie

i rewatched the simpson movie for no reason but the idea of having a good time cuz i watched before and remember it was very funny. it IS funny. however, something went beyond that i did not realize in the first watch (or i possibly forget bout it). i don't know that i should take this movie too serious but it is bout the journey of homer simpson himself envolved from being very selfish or narcissist or unaware of the surrounding, let's just call it "homer", to someone else less "homer".

let's say before his Epiphany, the movie is unbearable to watch every scene including him. the way he interact with his family (bart, marge,...) and also with other characters with no common sense is very repulsive. yes, it's a very strong word but it is true. a part of me yelling that i missed the fun and homer's characteristic is essence to show. Just take a break and see 2 things homer did in this movie. treating bart like fucking nobody, strangling him, not spilling the truth he dared. then literally, adopting a fucking pig. homer repeatingly doing the bad things with no remorse or even realized those are bad deeds. we saw bart questioning his life and about the father figure he had in compared to flanders. That is very sad, but the movie is like a rollercoster moving fast not leaving us with any of those ideas in our head, masking those questions with the FUN of the show. and of course, like any amateur average movie watcher, i must pause to take a break to process what happen. all credits must go to them makers of the movie. they done a excellent job at make it so vividly fun to watch as i was younger and painfully when im now more mature.

in conclusion, homer had no redeeming quality. Not until his epiphany, you know. hero saving the day motive is not new but that not the point though. i think as a younger self, this part is all i could ever paying attention to. like a very passive watcher i let the movie takes me to its final act without questioning it and overlook at the real problem or conflict of family life
