A disappointing season finale after a great second season

The whole season was a huge bless to watch. Fantastically written with a great and unusual depth for a comedy. But what happened in the season finale?

Suddenly, some plot holes cheated their way in, which remained completely unanswered. For example, where did Nate's gray hair suddenly come from? Why didn't anyone react?

The reason why Nate betrayed Ted was rather inadequate. When Nate says Ted dropped him, that's a reason. But where exactly did Ted drop him? I never had the feeling that Ted or anyone else did it. Okay, it was Roy's employment as an assistant coach, maybe. But was it the turning point for Nate? It wasn't explained at at all, but it could have been explained just in one sentence. But that didn't happen.

And the time jumps at the end I found also rather disturbing.

Really a pity, because otherwise the entire season was a ten out of ten.

nate's hair turned gray was like killing 2 birds with 1 stone. first, we realized how hard nate had to work for the club, giving his best knowledge and come up with best tactic every game in order for the club to win and end up getting promoted to PL. his dedication is not show enough. second, his hair turned completely silver in the final scene as West Ham Coach marked a milestone from a naive, clueless but with a very kind heart Nate (the great) into competent but mean, complacent and betrayal person. that's a fucking significant but subtle journey throughout 2 seasons.

is the reason why Nate betrayed Ted wasn't explained or portrayed enough? (short answer YES, but hear me out) as nate said, ted made him a most important person in the world and deserved all attention but only attention Nate need was from ted. the missing portrayal of when nate doesn't get what he want or ted abandons nate was not actually missing at all because THERE IS ABSOLUTELY NOTHING TO SHOW. allow me to explain, the dynamic between these two is completely off since the first ss, and the distance between them are further than ever even tho they work in the same office and talk to each other almost everyday. the producer instead of wasting time dig deeper into this silent conflict, they neglect showing directly to your face as well as ted neglecting nate. u should figure it out yourself with your sensitivity (or being in this kinda relationship yourself to experience). in my opinion, this is brilliant from the producer because let's be real, there are shit ton of aspects and problem to cover in a football club and the ultimate goal is to have good result. your personal matters are not matter to be prioritized even tho it's so so harsh for anyone to endure.

i was mad at nate. i don't hate him, i hate what he becomes.

the very same situation of a man having his personal issues (mental illness) but still manages to do the right thing and make the right decision is Ted all along (or even coach Beard). after coaching football at day, he had to face his insecurities, anxiety and depression whenever he get off from work. how the fuck should he spend his time giving nate his attention üò¢ his struggling to battle all of his issues in life and still being a very professional and maintain good spirit and hopefulness are highlight of the show for me.

karma police, i'm giving all i can but it's not enough.

btw, there are few reviews bout the so being preachy and should had stayed as comedy. first, funny witty humor only works once, twice or a few more times before people getting tired of it in a long running show. using humor in the right time and the right place as the foundation to explore more deeper into many other subjects is fucking brilliant. and the part of the show being preachy, when i don't want to be lectured, i don't listen to it. The soundest decision i will make when i am being preached by a TV show is i turn it off. if u want purely comedy, just watch the Ted Lasso sketches back in the days. they are so hilarious but watching them over and over again will make me sick. ted lasso series made a very long way from the gigs and i like it.
