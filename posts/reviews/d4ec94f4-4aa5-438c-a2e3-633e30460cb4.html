your name.

i was so bored in the quarantine and decided to buy some books to read during the time. After a while of browsing and selecting, i decided to buy 2. one of them is the "your name.". this novel is categorized as a light novel and idk light novel is a genre or not. i picked this book because i already watched the movie few year ago and since i did not remember much about it then this is a good chance to also revisit the movie if the book was any good. And yes, the book is awesome and also the movie.

Im gonna do the review the book and the movie as a whole. Like i said, i had already watched the movie a long time ago but still not remember anything quite significant to plot. however, when i read the book, it was all comming back to my mind and it kept me guessing what comming up next. the author/director of the book and the movie said the book is complementary to the movie and vice versa and i think this is absolutely the truth in this case. both of the movie and book are very faithful to each other, i could say they are identical but execute in different takes. we watch the movie in the third person perspective but in the the book we get to see the first person point of view from both Mitsuha and Taki. this really helps enhancing the experience of the audience like me. not only I get to see how both of this main characters feelings and thoughts to the surrounding environment but also see some of the questions i made when reading being answered when i watch.

first and foremost, the movie/book has 2 concepts to rely upon, body swap and time travel(?). 2 main characters are Mitsuha (a girl from the remote village) and Taki (a boy from the big city Tokyo) wake up with their body swapped only when they sleep. they both first believed all of those sudden events of body swapping was just a weired dreams because like most of our dreams, they all start to fade away so quickly when we wake up. after getting used to the body swapping while falling asleep, they started to see and explore every aspect of the life that the other live. all of those "dreams" happened for sometime until disaster came, that is when the second concept really play an important role. their bodies are swapped but from the very different period of time. Mitsuha from 2013 (im not very sure bout this fact) is connected to Taki's body in 2 years later - 2015. this is the part that confused me in the first watch because the movie progress with fast pacing that my mind was prepare for it, it raised a lot of question but in this second time i revisit the book and the movie, it all makes sense. im not going through all the detail because it will take forever and it also really depends on the audience focus to understand.

although of all concepts that we often see in Hollywood block-buster movies, this Japanese anime has integrated their very rich/deep culture and also reflect the state or contrast between modern but bustling and quiet/peaceful but boring life in countryside in Japan. as I stated above, we got a chance to explore all through Mitsuha and Taki perspective when they are put in the opposite environment.

the romance between 2 characters is just something very subtle and innocent first love. I myself is not a fan of overtly romantic stories and find myself happy with the romance in this movie. it is a tip of the story but also plays an part that connect 2 character and slowly blossom between them could even fully realized it. the love they had for each other really help them to overcome the reality that none of them could remember the other after the doomsday event but even find themselves constantly looking for that one until like 3 year later. Like musubi, the flow is fall apart but in the end rejoin together.

One thing i notice after watching this movie is the similarity between this one and the movie Woodjob. both of the movies show how Japanese people look at and value their culture and tradition. doing so, they are raising awareness for the young generation to step up and and take responsibility to preserve the culture. we saw how Taki and Yuki, both are clueless city boys live their best lives in remote area, learning the to value and respect culture that will remain hidden forever to them if they never actually be there. both movies did a really good job at showing and explaining the why people from old generation worship and faithful to their traditions, and also introducing the myth, religious customs, holy spirits and gods. on the other hand though, we saw how desperate Mitsuha is when she find herself stuck in this remote and quiet village with lacking of fancy restaurants and cafes, she dreams of breaking free and living in a big city Tokyo with fast-paced life style and probably never getting bored or running out of things to do. However, underneath all of that, we sympathize the loneliness she is living in Itomori, with 2 best friends and small family and the absent of the both mom and dad. she took the responsible of entire family and village since the very young age. the worst part of it is always being look at with the judging eyes from her peers.

in conclusion. it's good, go watch it and not being lazy to explore more aspects, answer your all questions and feeling reward doing so because in the end, it's all making sense.
