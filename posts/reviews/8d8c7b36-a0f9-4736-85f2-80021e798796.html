Victoria

Victoria was something truly different, yes, one-take wonder fills your heart with exhilarated feeling and also break your fucking heart. yeah, this is no shit. there are many insignificant people in the world, they went to club to fill the void of being forgotten like the Victoria in this one. so many questionable decisions were made by the main character in this movie. they/she left their lives in the hand of fate to decide. fate brought her everything but also took a away everything, left her only with the "golden ticket" and a fucking broken heart. the "golden ticket" wasn't exactly what she is looking for tho. she want to be noticed, she want to be treated with kindness, not indifference and on top of that, she want to be loved. Yeah, she once had an idea of that (in the coffee shop when the cam pan closely to her face for a short while) but then she got none of them. there are none of protagonist/antagonist in this movie, just characters getting involved deeper and deeper in the story. no realization or correction or overcoming oneself to become better version of themselves in this one, just plain rush, joy then pain and tears. there are a little calm yet excited moment when they left the car after the robbery and went to the club, foreshadowing the most brutal ending is so near. as my first watch goes, i know something's not right happening, like they went back to the start when things were still fun and innocent. only with this time, their dreamy montage at the club was so high that i fooled myself to think of an happy ending.

my favorite scene (yes, this is one-take though) was the part Blinker having a nervous breakdown or panic attack, he kept saying "my heart, i can't breath, stop the car". i was shock and frightening of how real and mentally it is for someone about to commit a bank robbery that their heart racing to the moon.

we acted reckless (myself included) once in a while just to feel something different, not much of consideration for the consequence, hoping it turns out just fine and maybe life-changing moment comes to us. but fuck no, life fucks us so hard, like swallowing a bitter pill that keep stuck in the throat. YES, lesson learned but what if i don't want that lesson at the beginning at all, "bitch"!

Victoria said 90% of us are wasting time (practicing piano 7 hours a day [if more than that then your hands become very painful], and dreaming of being an artist ever since a child). society treats those 90% so harsh, they turn into oblivion, dull and uninspired, working for money in order to perform basic human's function (like i am right now).

what about their feeling doesn't matter or what, they are also human being, not so significant one but dominance in number.
