Sometimes, life is a list of what we lost

i watched this one bc when i first saw the trailer of it with kevin costner and diane lane as an old spouses, i instantly thought of man of steel as they both portraited as mama and papa kent nurturing the iconic superman. i think they won my heart in that movie, especially kevin when him had a scene in the basement with young clark, explaining how originally they found clark. the way kevin delivered the line "you are my son" in response to clark saying "can i just pretend to be your son" was very emotional. as we knew in MoS, papa kent was stuck in the tornado while trying to rescued his family's dog, and the moment that he knows he could not make it out, he signaled his son to stay and remain his hidden identity. that was a self sacrifice in exchange for a lesson. what i was trying to say is how i love their characters and want to see it more in this one, a very different movie

the movie starts off fairly quick to introduce the narrative but goes on quite slowly. however, as slow as the pacing, the more tension is built up to it, then resolved in the grand tragedy at the end.

the weboy family is a terrifying. blanche is maniac and tyranny woman with her idea of keeping her family together. she's very repulsive to strangers (george and margaret) and manipulative to her family members. how brutal and cold-blood for a woman could be to order her own son to chop someone's hand off. in the end, that woman killed her entire family as she accidentally shot her cousin and other 2 sons while fighting george.

this blanche character reminds me of the norman bates's dead mother in Psycho (1960), the overprotective parents in Dogtooth (2009) and Mother Gothel in Tangled. they all share a thing in common where they has the idea of keeping their children and family as the asset to satisfy their own selfishness.

all member in weboy family including her 3 sons, cousin, daughter-in-laws and her grandson must obey blanche. they are the victim of her and i know it bc donnie (her son) showed reluctant as she told him to chop george fingers off. bill (her cousin) actually shocked and show a little bit of compassion after seeing that (he gave geogre and margaret a towel to cover the wound) and later stood himself in front of george and jimmy while blanche was pointing the gun at them.

a bit deeper to the mama boy - donnie. he's awful husband and stepfather when we saw him treat lorna and jimmy with violence. however, donnie himself was just a product of weboy family, i bet he had his a very unfortunate childhood being raised by an abusive mother who uses violence and intimidation as the way. we also learned that donnie did got away from his family once, but later being force to go back and live with his mother.

the side story with the 'indian' boy was also tragedy, an native american, speaking perfectly english with no accent but he cannot understand what their family talks. we learned that he is took away from family since 8 by the government to a school where he is brainwashed to forget his root. he chose to run away from that and live in middle of the hill. again, 1960s, racism, discrimination, segregation and so on.

some of the saddest scenes includes george lifted his dead son james and laid him on the horse's and carry him home. another scene when he had to 'retire' his family's horse named strawberry by shooting a bullet to her head. also a scene at the near end that george died after the second bullet in the chest and margaret whispered somethings to his ears.

this movie is set in the 60s in Montana and North Dakota with terrific nature in background along with retro cars, so the first thing i could think of is the movie Badlands (1973) (i love this one :). Irrelevant but i don't care, it's just the vibe. one last thing i noticed is that George - the kevin coster's character wearing the trucker denim jacket with shearling collar. i believe that he wears the same thing his little screen time in both MoS and BvS.
