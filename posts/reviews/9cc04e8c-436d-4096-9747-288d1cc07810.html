The Great Gatsby

the second book in the package i ordered is "The Great Gatsby". Unlike "your name.", i did not watched or read this one before. it's quite opposite to the other one when i already had the slightest idea when i read, this one i don't have any clue.

'It's called greed, old sport' - Jay Gatsby

i really don't know what to start with because my experience with book/movie is really like a roller-coaster, going up and down so fast and myself being immersive into the age of Jazz as the author Fitzgerald describes it.

i recommend you listen to this movie soundtrack (both OST and orchestral album) while reading this or at least check out 3 songs: 100$ Bill - JayZ, Young and Beautiful (very popular and nostalgic song) by Lana Del Rey and Together by The xx. those song really capture the most of the theme :) i will dive a bit into soundtrack of this movie because they're really good and this movie is set in the age of Jazz so why not

okay, let us start with what Nick - the narrator of the book and the movie talks about his life-lesson received from his dad: "whenever you feel like to criticizing anyone, just remember that all people in this world haven't had the advantages that you've had". it's kinda confusing to me about this but later, 'the advantages' is unfolded when Nick 'snobbishly' repeated his father's words. 'a sense of fundamental decencies is parcelled out unequally at birth'. thus, i could assume that the advantages is equated to a sense of fundamental decencies. the reason behind all of those big words is lying through out the book/movie. various of characters in the book exposed their true natures by their acts but all boiled down to the question of do they have their fundamental decencies or not?

'I was within and without, simultaneously enchanted and repelled by inexhaustible variety of life' - Nick Carraway

there are a lot of characters to explore. Nick the narrator with his strong moral but innocent/naïve is brought into new york 1920s. he is some what of the mirror reflect everyone's nature but also being smeared in the era where moral was loosening. Nick is quite plain and simple in contrast to Gatsby, a very mysterious and complex person, which is reflected by Nick through his narration, we saw a lot of aspects around this 'Oxford man'. this pair with their friendship being built throughout the book/movie are really the highlight, they are complementary to each other in different ways but in general, i think both of them stand out to represent what is good and right as a human should be. let's dissect Gatsby, as the outside world sees, he's the man, the myth, the legend. A true gentleman with his respectable manor and one among the wealthiest self-made business men. He is the representation of the new money class. he threw the dazzling grand party in his mansion so generously that everyone who is invited or not (mostly uninvited) just come without even acknowledge the host. the very funny scene in the movie where Nick was cluelessly holding his invitation card asking everyone he encountered at the party asking for Gatsby. However, as the story unveiled, there is nothing respectable about this noble man Jay Gatsby.

"He's a crooked, George" - Tom Buchanan

Gatsby himself was actually a bootlegger, selling alcohol during the Prohibition period, along side with Meyer Wolfsheim aka 'the gambler, the gangster, the one who fixed the 1919 World's Series'. He is of course not originated from a honorable but just a dirt-poor family. the special part about Jay Gatsby(originally named James Gatz) is he never see himself so ordinary

'My life has got to be like this, it got to keep going up' - Jay Gatsby
'I don't want you to think i was just some nobody' - JG

Jay found himself to become a greater man, from the time with Dan Cody, he learned to be a gentleman. However, a gentleman is just a shell when he had no money. Then comes the time he joined the army and headed to WW1, heroically in the war, he still penniless after that but in his ambitious mind, he set the goal that he would only return from Oxford, England after he made something for himself. So what is the real motivation behind Jay, isn't just because he is so ambitious to climb the social ladder. an turning event (a completed reincarnation) that forever changed his life was the time he met the love of his life - Daisy right before he was deployed.

'I always knew that i could climb, I could only climb when i climb alone' - JG

Daisy herself was a girl originated from a upper class family. she is lovely, charming, her seductive but innocent voice and dreamy eyes always captivate everyone around her (myself included). when Jay headed to war but could not come back to her, she decided not to wait anymore from him and married to Tom Buchanan, a very wealthy heir, a sportive 'polo player' ('not the polo player 😠'), a jealousy husband with his not so secretive affair.

'Her voice is... is full of money' - JG

However to Jay, Daisy was never just his first love but his only love. 5 years later and Jay's still looking for Daisy and doing everything (including throwing those big ass parties) to and catch Daisy attention and meet her again. He set up everything so perfectly through Nick (Daisy's cousin) and Jordan (Daisy's friend). Eventually they see each other again and from there an affair between them blossom so naturally. Sadly, from this point we witness the downfall of the Great Gatsby. as Nick observation, Daisy to Gatsby's imagination was beyond the real Daisy

'Rich girl doesn't marry poor boy' - some fucker that i don't remember his name

His grand vision for the life of him and her, his perfect irresistible imagination and his incorruptible dream, all of them kill him, making him blind. Nick already said to Jay: 'You can't repeat the past' but him not believing it. the green light across the bay separated between West Egg (Jay/Nick) and East Egg (Daisy/Tom) was Gatsby's vain beacon of relentless hope. As nowadays speaking, Jay Gatsby is truly a simp, a legendary simp, a good one tho.

'The only thing respectable 'bout you, old sport, is your money. I have as same as you so that's mean we are equal' - Jay to Tom
'Oh no, we were born differently. It is in our blood' - Tom then replied to Jay

in contrast to Jay from West Egg is Tom (and Daisy) from East Egg. If Jay is referred as newly rich/new money then Tom is absolutely opposite - old money type. Nick lives next to Jay and Jordan lives with Tom/Daisy act like a bridge between these people, they are both within and without. the scene in the Plaza, where everyone including Tom, Daisy, Jordan, Nick and Jay together in a room. the genius part of this idea bring the tension and push it to the maximum when the stories eventually unfold one by one with confrontation and no hesitation. Tom and Jay fight for their Daisy. Tom exposed Jay with his shady business. Jay said Daisy never love Tom, she only married to him because Gatsby was poor. And Daisy, she was emotional and indecisive when she stated she love Tom but she also loves Jay too when Jay persuaded her to say she was never in love with Tom. at this point, after reading the book and watch the adaptation, i know her decision when Jay and Daisy left the Plaza. Tom knew he had won and probably Jay knew it too. However, Jay still hold on the the hope, the mirage that he and the women he loves together. All of the events lead to tragic deaths

the line 'her voice is full of money' said by Jay is in the book but omitted in the movie. the director Baz Luhrmann explained he shot the scene but cut it out because he want all the characters going to Plaza scene with the maximum tension, and not showing any clue that Jay some what spot a slightest of him losing the fight. i don't blame the director, he had good reason for it and for the most of deleted scenes. however, i watch that scene and that was done brilliantly by LeoD.

'I haven't made use of the pool all summer' x2 - JG

i don't want to go into the end too much because it is so fucking tragic and sad, i watched the movie twice in 2 days and CRIED twice. like, seriously, the scene at the pool where Gatsby swimming and waiting for the call from Daisy is so touching to my core. The moment the phone rang, Gatsby rose from the water with hopefulness and happiness in his face, then the shot, the realization, the blood spreading in his chest, the acceptance in the final moment. Thanks to a comment on Youtube points out that Gatsby died in happiness knowing Daisy had chosen him over Tom but in the cruelest fact, the call is from Nick, Daisy never call then leave East Egg without bothering ever again. this scene is so brilliantly subtle with it meanings underneath. Because I rooted for Gatsby so much (and i think all of us do), i watched that scene with the lump in my throat, tears in my eyes and anger/resentment in my head. However there is nothing i can do but sit and watch his body slowly drowning in the pool. one more thing i discover is that the color of the pool is kinda emerald/turquoise like, it instantly made me thing of the green light symbol to Gatsby. for me, i would say with his death, his dream was fulfilled as he floating in the the green light (yes, im aware that im turning into somewhat a literature teacher here!).

back to the question of does this people had their sense of fundamental decencies or not. to be short, apart from the narrator Nick, none of them have with their morals being corrupted. but in the case of Gatsby, we understand and sympathize him as Nick does. Nick calls Gatsby is the only exception he made when it come to morality, because deep down, Jay Gatsby has his own core values to follow, they are reasonable and drive Gatsby, alongside of the ambitious mentality achieve the American dream even using the most notorious ways, but that flashy dream was just a cover for the truly pure love he hides underneath.

'They're a rotten crowd. You worth the whole damn bunch put together' - Nick to Jay

I'm now talk a bit about the movie itself. I really love this adaptation. it has some of the flashiest madness of sound and visual, Jazzy vibes too, not to mention JayZ. The movie shot mostly in digital and scenes are created artificially with SFX but the producers bring in the grainy film texture to it and making it feel old but still vividly 1920s. Nick said he is only drunk twice, and in the second time we witnessed he's fucking popping pills, cannot blame the naïve Nick entirely but he had a very great line coming from this filthy crazy montage: '...chemical madness, a willingness of the heart that burst thunderously upon us all...'. thanks to movie we get the chance to be amazed by the splendid look of the JG's mansion. From the inside, we see the interior is decorated delicately with golden art deco style arch's ceiling, wall and floor. this movie was also very stylish in term of costume from that era. Before the tour to JG's mansion we have a loveliest scene in the nick tiny house made out 'cardboard that only cost 80 a month'. i love how clumsy and nervous Gatsby was before the actual talking with Daisy, how Nick awkwardly felt out of the place between them in the room full of orchids of every kind. this Nick Carraway by Tobey Maguire hilariously reminds me of his other role of Peter Parker aka Spider-man for a bit.

the scene that i would love to see from the book but disappoint when i don't find it in the movie or those deleted scene of the movie is the scene Nick in the car and Jordan driving and Jordan said: 'I hate careless people. That's why i like u'. fucking lovely conversation between them. but as the director said the romance between them is cut out because we want the audience to engage fully into Jay and Daisy romance. other scene that happened in the book but not in the movie could be found in the featurette where all the deleted scenes are shot but never make it to the final version and i'm happy to watch all of them.

the soundtrack for this movie is brilliant because it's not only about Jazz but also infuse the Hip Hop material into it, making the soundtrack something old and nostalgia but still relevant to today. the book reflect what going on in 1920s but irony all of them still matter in today life, that is what i think JayZ tried to tell us through some of his songs. Jazzy songs for the parties scenes to depict the flashy of sound and visual. finally those orchestral songs (mostly remixes of Together - the xx and Young and Beautiful - Lana del Rey) is absolutely banger for mostly romantic scenes.
